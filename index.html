Unbanked and Underbanked Americans Living in Poverty

Unbanked and Underbanked Americans Living in Poverty

One of the standard definitions of poverty for the USA is when a family of four is living on an annual income of less than $25,200, or an individual is living at or below the threshold of $12,760. By that standard, almost forty-million Americans live in poverty, and tens of millions more Americans hover at or near that invisible indicator line. About 70% of adults say they are struggling to make ends meet. According to the FDIC, at least 7 million Americans are unbanked and about 50 million are considered underbanked. On the global poverty scale, while the USA is third largest by population, it is ranked a surprising 55th amongst all nations for poverty, according to United Nations data. The reported poverty rate is an astonishing 17.8%. Exact numbers will vary according to definitions, reporting reliability, and report objectives, but no one will dispute that the number of financially struggling Americans is very large.

Lifting Families and Communities Out of Poverty

TZT Holdings, with its TranzactCard financial ecosystem, has been formed as a social impact enterprise to drive major increases in the purchasing power of those who need it most. TranzactCard has adopted former Harvard professor, Clayton M. Christensen’s final book as its own handbook for how to use innovation to lift families and communities out of poverty. Consistent with those principles, TranzactCard resisted early inclinations to operate as a charitable foundation, opting instead to conduct its operations as a for-profit so that it would not have to be continuously obsessed with raising money which is the bane of most not-for-profits. Though established as a for-profit enterprise, TranzactCard has no shareholders or members; the LLC membership interests are held in trust; the enterprise is governed by a board of directors supported by a diverse and talented advisory council.
